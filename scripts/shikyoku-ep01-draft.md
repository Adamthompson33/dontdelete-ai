### NOTES ON DIRECTION

**Visual language for the "normal" sections:**
- Warm, golden lighting. Soft focus on backgrounds. The school looks *beautiful*.
- Camera moves are smooth, stable â€” Lilith's world feels safe.
- But: every wide shot has at least one camera visible in frame. The audience sees them even when Lilith doesn't. This is Hitchcockian â€” the audience knows something the character doesn't.

**Visual language for the surveillance sections:**
- Cold. Blue-green. Flat. Monitor resolution â€” slightly pixelated, slightly wrong.
- Dr. Luke's control room is lit only by screens. Her face in monitor-glow.
- The transition between "Lilith's world" and "the truth" should feel like changing channels.

**Sound design:**
- The "normal" school has ambient sound: birds, wind, laughter, distant music from rehearsal rooms.
- The surveillance rooms have a LOW HUM â€” the frequency from the cold open. Air conditioning. Hard drives. The sound of being watched.
- When Lilith has her "flicker" moments, ALL ambient sound drops out for a beat. Silence. Then rushes back.

**The cold open fight:**
- Choreographed to feel effortless. These girls aren't struggling. They're bored. That's what makes it scary.
- The violence should be technically precise but emotionally casual â€” they've done this hundreds of times.
- Play it for dark comedy. The straw throw, the TV show deadline, the cat drawing. They're kids with terrifying skills who think this is just... Tuesday.
